Understanding Cancer

Uncontrolled Cell Growth

Cancer occurs when abnormal cells multiply rapidly without regulation.

Tumor Formation

Abnormal cells accumulate forming masses that invade surrounding tissues.

Metastasis

Cancer can spread to distant organs through blood or lymphatic systems.

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer develops in breast tissue, particularly in milk ducts or lobules. It is one of the most common cancers among women worldwide.

  • Lump or thickening in breast
  • Change in breast size or shape
  • Skin dimpling or redness
  • Nipple discharge
  • Persistent breast pain

Cervical Cancer

Cervical cancer develops in the cervix and is primarily caused by HPV infection.

  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Unusual vaginal discharge
  • Pelvic pain

Detection Methods

Clinical Examination

Doctors check for lumps and abnormalities.

Mammography

X-ray imaging used for early detection of breast cancer.

Pap Smear

Test that detects abnormal cervical cells early.

Common Symptoms

Lump in breast

Breast swelling

Abnormal vaginal bleeding

Pelvic pain
